## 摘要
动力系统的研究长期以来一直专注于其渐近动力学的表征，例如不动点、极限环和其他类型的吸引子，以及这些不变集如何随系统参数的变化而改变其性质。然而，最近，瞬态动力学的重要性，特别是长瞬态及其之间的顺序转换，在包括生态学、神经科学和细胞生物学在内的各个领域得到了越来越多的认可。在长瞬态的几种可能起源中，幽灵吸引子（ghost attractors）因其在非自治环境中有趣的动力学特性、新的理论进展以及越来越多在经验上表现出与幽灵吸引子一致的动力学系统而受到特别关注。尽管人们对一般的瞬态动力学，特别是幽灵吸引子的兴趣日益浓厚，但与渐近动力学相比，可供研究人员分类和表征其潜在机制的理论概念和软件工具明显较少。为了填补这一空白，我们将鞍结点（saddle-nodes）推广到高维中心流形，并为它们的幽灵吸引子提供了一个定义。随后，我们引入了专门用于识别和表征幽灵吸引子及其复合结构（如幽灵通道和幽灵循环）的算法，并展示了如何利用这些概念和算法，对以生命系统为重点的广泛系统模型的瞬态动力学获得新的见解，例如，能够描述幽灵的分岔。这些算法已在 Python 中实现，并作为 PyGhostID 提供，这是一个用户友好的开源软件包。

## Abstract
The study of dynamical systems has long focused on the characterization of their asymptotic dynamics such as fixed points, limit cycles and other types of attractors and how these invariant sets change their properties as systems parameters change. More recently, however, the importance of transient dynamics, especially of long transients and sequential transitions between them, has been increasingly recognized in various fields including ecology, neuroscience and cell biology. Among several possible origins of long transients, ghost attractors have received particular attention due to interesting dynamical properties in non-autonomous settings, new theoretical developments, and an increasing number of systems that empirically show dynamics consistent with ghost attractors. Despite this growing interest in transient dynamics generally and ghost attractors in particular, there are significantly fewer theoretical concepts and software tools available to researchers to classify and characterize their underlying mechanisms compared to asymptotic dynamics. To address this gap, we generalize saddle-nodes to account for higher-dimensional center manifolds and provide a definition for their ghost attractors. We then introduce algorithms to specifically identify and characterize ghost attractors and their composite structures such as ghost channels and ghost cycles and show how these concepts and algorithms can be used to gain new insights into the transient dynamics of a wide range of system models focusing on living systems, allowing, e.g., to describe bifurcations of ghosts. The algorithms are implemented in Python and available as \tt PyGhostID, a user-friendly open-source software package.
