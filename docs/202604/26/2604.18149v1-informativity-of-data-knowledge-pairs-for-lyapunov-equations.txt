# Informativity of Data-Knowledge Pairs for Lyapunov Equations 

Ikumi Banno 

Abstract — In the past few years, data informativity with prior knowledge has attracted increasing attention. This line of research aims to characterize a dataset on a dynamical system that enables system analysis or design only by the dataset and given prior knowledge on the system. In this paper, we investigate such a characterization for the data-driven problem of computing a unique solution to Lyapunov equations. First, we introduce a notion of joint informativity for data-knowledge pairs as an extension of the standard informativity concept. Second, we derive an algebraic equivalent condition for the joint informativity. Finally, we provide further insights into the joint informativity by considering a special case of prior knowledge. The characterization presented in this paper is developed for a wide class of prior knowledge, enabling the incorporation of various forms of system information. 

I. INTRODUCTION Data-driven approaches [1]–[3] have emerged as one of a promising paradigm for the analysis and control of real-world dynamical systems. In many practical situations, the system dynamics involve unknown parameters or may be entirely unknown, which makes it difficult to directly apply standard model-based techniques. To overcome this limita-tion, data-driven approaches utilize measurement data for system analysis and control without requiring an explicit model. These frameworks can be broadly classified into two categories: indirect methods and direct methods [2]. The former is based on system identification followed by standard model-based methods, whereas the latter analyzes or designs dynamical systems directly from data, bypassing explicit system identification. In analysis and design of physical systems, incorporating prior knowledge on the system dynamics into a data-driven framework plays an important role [3]. One advantage is that it can help reduce the amount of data required for data-driven tasks. Another advantage is that the incorporation of prior knowledge can improve the reliability and explainability of the obtained results. For these reasons, data-driven methods that incorporate prior knowledge have a long history of study. In particular, such approaches have been well developed in the context of indirect methods, where gray-box identi-fication [3] has been established. In contrast, direct data-driven methods have mainly been developed for black-box 

settings [4]–[6], where prior knowledge is not incorporated. However, some works have considered direct methods with prior knowledge, e.g., network reconstruction [7], robust control [8], and model predictive control [9].

Recently, data informativity has been proposed as a novel framework for direct data-driven methods [2], [10], [11]. This concept characterizes whether a given dataset contains sufficient information to solve a data-driven problem of in-terest. Within this paradigm, conditions under which data are informative for various analysis and control tasks have been investigated [10]–[18]. In particular, Lyapunov equations, a fundamental framework for system analysis and control, have been studied in this context by the authors [14], [15]. These results are effective in the absence of prior knowledge; however, they may be conservative when additional system information beyond measurement data is available. This motivates the need to characterize data-knowledge pairs, rather than datasets alone, that enable system analysis and design. In this viewpoint, data informativity with prior knowledge has begun to attract attention [16]–[18] since 2025. In partic-ular, [16], [17] consider prior knowledge on system-theoretic properties: [17] characterizes datasets enabling stability anal-ysis under the assumption of system positivity, while [16] studies data-driven stabilization under prior knowledge such as stabilizability or controllability. In contrast, [18] considers a different type of prior knowledge, namely that some entries of the system matrices are exactly known, and applies it to data-driven stabilization of polynomial systems. However, characterizations of data-knowledge pairs for general classes of prior knowledge remain limited in the data informativity framework. Moreover, such characterizations for Lyapunov equations have never been explored so far. Therefore, this paper characterizes data-knowledge pairs for general classes of prior knowledge in data-driven sys-tem analysis, focusing on the problem of determining a unique solution to Lyapunov equations. First, we introduce an informativity notion for data-knowledge pairs for the problem. This notion, referred to as joint informativity in this paper, can be regarded as an extension of the standard data informativity in [10]. Next, we derive an algebraic condition equivalent to our joint informativity under a mild assumption on the prior knowledge. This condition is characterized by the existence of a solution to a system of Lyapunov equations determined by the dataset and the prior knowledge. Based on this result, we also provide a data-driven method for solving Lyapunov equations. Finally, we present additional insights into joint informativity by considering a structured class of prior knowledge. The proposed framework is illustrated through numerical examples.

CONCLUSIONS AND FUTURE WORKS In this paper, we introduced a notion of joint informativity for a pair consisting of a dataset and prior knowledge, and provided a necessary and sufficient condition for the pair to be jointly informative for the Lyapunov equation. We showed that the joint informativity is characterized by the existence of a solution to a system of Lyapunov equations determined by the dataset and the prior knowledge. Furthermore, we explored joint informativity in a special case where the prior knowledge has a specific structure. The notion of joint informativity should be further inves-tigated for other data-driven tasks in system analysis and controller design. It would also be interesting to consider experimental design problems under the presence of prior knowledge. In the context of Lyapunov equations, more general settings deserve further study.
