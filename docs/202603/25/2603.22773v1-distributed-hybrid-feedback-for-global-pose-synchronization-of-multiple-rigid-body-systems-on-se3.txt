Abstract: This paper investigates the problem of pose synchronization for multiple rigid body systems evolving on the matrix Lie group SE(3). We propose a distributed hybrid feedback control scheme with global asymptotic stability guarantees using relative pose and group velocity measurements. The key idea consists of constructing a new potential function on SE(3) × R

with a generalized non-diagonal weighting matrix, and a set of auxiliary scalar variables with continuous-discrete hybrid dynamics. Based on the new potential function and the auxiliary scalar variables, a geometric distributed hybrid feedback designed directly on SE(3) is proposed to achieve global pose synchronization. Numerical simulation results are presented to illustrate the performance of the proposed distributed hybrid control scheme. 

Keywords: Pose synchronization, Multiple rigid body systems, Lie group SE (3), Hybrid feedback 1. INTRODUCTION The design of decentralized control algorithms for pose (position and orientation) synchronization of multiple rigid body systems (MRBSs) is crucial in many practical applications, such as robotics (Bullo et al., 2009), satellites (Subbarao and Welsh, 2008), unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs) (Rashad et al., 2020) and spacecraft (Lawton and Beard, 2002). Since the orientation/attitude of a rigid body evolves on the Special Orthogonal group SO(3), the motion of a rigid body in three-dimensional space inher-ently evolves on the Special Euclidean group SE(3). There are several typical approaches that address the coordina-tion of MRBSs in three-dimensional space. The typical one considers the decoupled control framework on SO(3) × R3,whose key idea lies in separating the rigid body dynamics into independent rotational dynamics and translational dynamics, and then designing independent feedback laws for the attitude control and the position control separately, see for instance, (Schlanbusch and Nicklasson, 2011; Nair and Leonard, 2007). These decoupled approaches funda-mentally disregard the intrinsic geometric coupling be-tween the rotational and translational dynamics inherent to the Lie group SE(3), leading to non-rigorous stability results. Other approaches relying on the dual quaternions

to parameterize SE(3) have been proposed in Filipe and Tsiotras (2013); Wang et al. (2012) and Malladi et al. (2020), which allows to represent the pose in a compact form without requiring decoupling. However, the unit-quaternion is known as a non-unique attitude representation (double cover of the space of rota-tions) and may cause the so-called unwinding phenomenon in practice (Mayhew et al., 2011). Few works consider the simultaneous coordination of attitude and position dynamics on the matrix Lie group SE(3), such as (Thun-berg et al., 2016; Hatanaka et al., 2011; Igarashi et al., 2008). The method proposed in Thunberg et al. (2016), leverages the Axis-Angle representation of the rotation to design the control laws. In Hatanaka et al. (2011) and Igarashi et al. (2008), a passivity-based pose synchroniza-tion approach on SE(3) has been proposed. However, the synchronization control schemes developed in Thunberg et al. (2016); Hatanaka et al. (2011) and Igarashi et al. (2008) fail to guarantee global asymptotic stability (GAS) due to the topological properties of the motion space con-taining SO(3). In fact, almost global asymptotic stability (AGAS) 1 is the strongest stability result that one can achieve, with continuous time-invariant feedback, in spaces involving SO(3).

To overcome the above-mentioned topological obstruction, a hybrid feedback framework (Goebel et al., 2009) on the matrix Lie group SO(3), relying on a “synergistic” family of potential functions, has been introduced in Mayhew and Teel (2011), leading to GAS guarantees. This hybrid feedback framework has been leveraged to solve the attitude tracking and estimation problems on SO(3) with GAS guarantees, see for instance, (Mayhew and Teel, 2013; Lee, 2015; Berkane and Tayebi, 2016; Wang and Tayebi, 2023). However, most of these hybrid approaches rely on the compactness of the group SO(3), which makes it difficult to extend them to the global pose control problem on the noncompact group SE(3). Recently, a new hybrid feedback scheme relying on an auxiliary scalar variable with hybrid dynamics and a suitable potential function on SO(3) × R (SE(3) × R) has been proposed in Wang and Tayebi (2021), which can achieve global attitude (pose) control on matrix Lie groups SO(3) (SE(3)). Inspired by this work, the authors in Boughellaba and Tayebi (2024) proposed a distributed attitude synchronization control scheme on SO(3) with GAS guarantees. However, the design of pose synchronization schemes directly on SE(3) with GAS guarantees remains an open problem. In this work, we consider the problem of pose synchroniza-tion for MRBSs on SE(3) using relative pose information. We first provide a systematic procedure for the construc-tion of a generic potential function on SE(3) × R that relaxes the requirement of a diagonal weighting matrix used in Wang and Tayebi (2021). Then, an extended syner-gistic potential function on (SE(3) × R)N is introduced for the pose synchronization problem. With this synergistic potential function, a new distributed hybrid feedback for pose synchronization of MRBSs is designed directly on the group SE(3) with GAS guarantees. 2. PRELIMINARIES

CONCLUSION We proposed a new distributed hybrid pose synchroniza-tion scheme, with GAS guarantees, on SE(3) over undi-rected, connected, and acyclic graphs. A new potential function on (SE(3) × R)M for a general weighting matrix 

A, involving a set of variables θk with flow and jump dynamics, was constructed to help with the design of the proposed hybrid control scheme. The proposed feedback control scheme, designed on Lie group SE(3), is restricted to fully actuated rigid body systems. Since many UAVs are underactuated (for instance, quadrotor UAVs), the pose control scheme proposed in this paper cannot be applied directly. Designing global pose synchronization schemes on SE(3), for underactuated rigid body systems, over general communication graph topologies, including directed graphs, is an interesting future work.
